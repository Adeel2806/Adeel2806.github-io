Bootcamp 2020: Earth and Moon scene

My first programming project, concluding the 2 week Programming Bootcamp at City (2020). This Processing project demonstrates a comprehensive understanding of the use of loops and randomness. I created a earth scene in which the earth spins and during this a pattern simialr to the pattern of a kleidascope is presented. The pattern continously changes and i did this to represent how the planet of earth keeps changing as it spins as the earth does not look the same all the way around it and where the land and ocens are does differ from time to time through things such as floods or sea rise. To create this pattern i used a series of balls that go around in a circle.These balls are attached to keep eathother in pairs to create multiple lines and as they spin these lines are what makes the pattern. I then used a 3D sphere to create the moon that would orbit the meterphorical earth at an equal distance. To do this i used a variable called Angle and a rotate function to make sure that the 'moon' is always at a equal distnace. Finally i also used randomness for the stars so that apeear in different locaitions as the earth and monn spin i did this to give it a more realistic feel as the earth that i created does not look the same to the earth that we are used to seeing. in phase two i plan to add more then one star into the background in random locaitions.
